\section{Research methodology}

The following table provides an overview of the selected scientific methodology based on the
conventional Research ‘Onion’ Model, presented by \citep{saunders2019research}.

\begin{table}[H]
    \centering
    \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.5}
    \begin{tabular}{|p{4.8cm}|p{10cm}|}
        \hline
        Research philosophy & 
        This study adopts pragmatism as its research philosophy, as it 
        is primarily focused on addressing a real-world problem, enhancing 
        fundamental analysis through an Agentic AI-based approach, rather 
        than adhering to a single philosophical paradigm. Pragmatism enables 
        the integration of both quantitative methods and qualitative insights, 
        allowing the research to effectively handle diverse data types. \\
        \hline
        Research approach & 
        An abductive research approach is employed, beginning with the 
        observation of limitations in conventional stock fundamental analysis 
        tools, and iteratively developing theoretical and practical insights 
        through the design and evaluation of an Agentic AI system. This 
        approach allows the research to move flexibly between data-driven 
        findings and theoretical reasoning throughout the development process. \\
        \hline
        Research methodological choice & 
        A mixed-methods design is adopted, combining quantitative analysis 
        of financial metrics with qualitative insights derived from news articles, management 
        commentary, and market narratives. This dual approach enables a 
        comprehensive evaluation of stock fundamentals that neither method 
        could achieve in isolation.  \\
        \hline
        Research strategy & 
        The research follows a design science strategy, centred on the 
        development and evaluation of an artefact, an Agentic AI-powered 
        fundamental analysis tool. The system is designed to autonomously 
        retrieve, process, and synthesise data, producing actionable investment insights. 
        Iterative prototyping and performance evaluation against financial reports, broker research reports and interviews with domain experts form the core of the research strategy. \\
        \hline
        Research time horizon & 
        A cross-sectional time horizon is adopted, with the tool evaluated 
        against a defined snapshot of stock market data within a specific 
        period. This allows for the systematic assessment of the tool's 
        analytical capabilities without requiring longitudinal tracking, 
        though future work may extend to longitudinal evaluation. \\
        \hline
        Research data collection and analysis & 
        Data is collected from multiple sources, including financial statements in CSE, news articles, analyst reports is analysed using natural language processing techniques 
        embedded within the Agentic AI pipeline. The tool's outputs are 
        evaluated based on accuracy, completeness, and relevance of the 
        generated fundamental analysis. \\
        \hline
    \end{tabular}
    \caption{Research Methodology}
    \label{tab:research-methodology}
\end{table}

\section{Development methodology}

This study adopts an iterative development methodology, where the proposed Agentic AI-based system is developed in incremental stages. Each iteration involves designing and implementing a prototype, followed by systematic evaluation of its performance in supporting fundamental analysis. The insights gained from each evaluation are used to refine and enhance the subsequent version of the system. This continuous cycle of development and improvement ensures that the solution evolves progressively, leading to a more effective and reliable framework for fundamental analysis. 

\section{Project management methodology}

Agile principles are adopted for project management to support iterative development and continuous improvement, enabling the system to evolve through successive refinements until it meets the desired project objectives.

\subsection{Project Scope}

This study focuses on fundamentally analysing publicly listed companies in the Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE). However, diversified holding companies are excluded due to the complexity arising from their multi-sector operations. The analysis is primarily based on annual reports, and quarterly reports are not considered. As a result, the findings may reflect a maximum time lag of up to one year.

\subsection{Resources}

\subsubsection{Software resources}


\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Agentic AI Framework} (LangChain / LangGraph) --- used 
    for orchestrating multi-step AI agent pipelines for autonomous 
    retrieval, reasoning, and synthesis of financial data.

    \item \textbf{Large Language Model} (OpenAI GPT-5.5 / Claude 4.6 Sonnet / AWS Textract) 
    --- used for extracting qualitative insights, summarising financial 
    narratives, and interpreting unstructured financial text.

    \item \textbf{Vector Database} (ChromaDB / Pinecone) --- used for 
    storing and retrieving embedded financial documents for 
    Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) within the agent pipeline.

    \item \textbf{Backend Development} (Python, FastAPI) --- used for 
    building the core application logic, API endpoints, and agent 
    orchestration layer.

    \item \textbf{NLP and Embeddings} (HuggingFace Transformers, OpenAI 
    Embeddings) --- used for generating text embeddings for semantic 
    search and qualitative document retrieval.

    \item \textbf{Frontend / Interface} (React) --- used for 
    developing the user interface for interacting with the 
    fundamental analysis tool.

    \item \textbf{Version Control} (Git, GitHub) --- used for source code 
    management and project versioning.

    \item \textbf{Development Environment} (Cursor, Claude Code, Jupyter Notebook) --- 
    used for code development, prototyping, and iterative testing 
    of AI pipelines.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{New technical skills}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Agentic AI Development} --- designing and implementing 
    multi-agent AI systems capable of autonomously planning, retrieving 
    information, reasoning over financial data, and generating structured 
    outputs using LangChain and LangGraph.

    \item \textbf{Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)} --- building RAG 
    pipelines that combine vector-based document retrieval with large 
    language model generation, enabling the agent to ground its qualitative and quantitative
    insights in financial documents.

    \item \textbf{Prompt Engineering} --- designing and optimising prompts 
    for large language models to produce accurate, structured, and 
    contextually relevant fundamental analysis outputs across 
    diverse stock profiles.

    \item \textbf{NLP for Financial Text} --- applying natural language 
    processing techniques (to financial reports, news articles, and 
    industry level reports), entity recognition, 
    and summarisation.

    \item \textbf{Qualitative Research Methods} --- designing and conducting 
    semi-structured interviews with domain experts to evaluate 
    AI-generated qualitative outputs, and thematically analysing 
    interview responses.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Data requirements}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Financial data} (CSE reports / regulatory reports) --- used for retrieving 
    historical financial statements, stock prices, and key financial ratios.

    \item \textbf{Qualitative data} (Reputed news sites, management interviews, macroeconomic reports, industry reviews) 
    --- used for collecting qualitative aspects of the company, its position in the market and future sector forecasts.

    \item \textbf{{Price}} (CSE web) --- used for retrieving 
    current market price.

    \item \textbf{{Research reports}} (Registered Stock Broker reports) --- used for validating computed quantitative values.
\end{itemize}
