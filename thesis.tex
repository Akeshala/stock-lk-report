\begin{abstractstyle}{\hsummary}

Fundamental analysis is the cornerstone of value-based investing, yet producing a thorough, well-structured analytical report on a company demands the time and expertise of a trained equity analyst, a resource that most retail investors cannot afford. The current generation of AI-powered investment tools has done little to close this gap: much of the published work concentrates on short-term price prediction, which the classical investment literature would classify not as investment but as speculation. This paper presents an Agentic AI system that produces template-based fundamental analysis reports for publicly listed companies. The system decomposes the analytical workflow into specialised agents, each responsible for a specific section of a standardised report template, including financial statement analysis, regulatory filing summarisation, macroeconomic contextualisation, and integrated valuation commentary. Templates serve a dual purpose: they enforce coverage of every dimension a competent analyst would consider, and they constrain the language model's output to grounded, comparable, and auditable claims rather than free-form recommendations. The system is built on a multi-agent orchestration framework with retrieval-augmented access to filings, earnings call transcripts, and macroeconomic data, and it deliberately separates numerical extraction from natural-language synthesis to mitigate hallucination risk. By focusing exclusively on the company's intrinsic situation rather than its price trajectory, the system places itself unambiguously on the investment side of the classical distinction. The contribution is a transparent, reproducible pipeline that makes structured fundamental analysis accessible to retail investors while preserving the auditability institutional users require.
